About

Ahmed Owais Durrani

I'm a PhD candidate in Sustainable Development and Climate Change at IUSS Pavia, with a background in Civil Engineering and Engineering Management. My work centres on quantitative climate risk modelling and data-driven decision support — developing, calibrating, and validating analytical models to assess physical climate risks, infrastructure resilience, and disaster impacts under uncertainty.

My research spans flood risk assessment, transport network disruption modelling, vulnerability analysis, cost-benefit assessment, and nature-based adaptation strategies. I work mostly in Python, building graph-based models that translate hazard data into concrete measures of who loses access to emergency services, and by how much, when a city floods.

Before Pavia, I completed an MS in Engineering Management at the Ghulam Ishaq Khan Institute and a BS in Civil Engineering at Balochistan University of Information Technology, Engineering, and Management Sciences — after which I worked as a site engineer, which is part of why I care about models that hold up against how infrastructure actually behaves on the ground.
