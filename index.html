PhD Candidate — IUSS Pavia

When floodwater rises, which routes still get through?

I study how pluvial flooding disrupts urban transport networks — the direct damage it causes, and the cascading costs that follow, from delayed emergency response to lost economic activity. Milan is my case study; green roofs are the mitigation strategy I test.

Focus areas

Three conditions, one network

My work compares a city's transport network across a free-flow baseline, a flooded network with no intervention, and a network mitigated by green roof implementation.

Pluvial flood modelling

Hydrological simulation of surface-water flooding and its effect on road network capacity and travel times.

Disruption impact analysis

Quantifying both the direct damage floods cause to infrastructure, and the indirect costs that follow — from delayed emergency response to lost economic activity.

Green roof mitigation

Testing nature-based solutions as a lever to restore network resilience — case study: Milan.

Impact framework

Where flood impacts actually land

Flood impacts are usually classified along two axes: whether they hit directly or indirectly, and whether they're tangible (measurable in monetary terms) or intangible. My work sits in the two tangible quadrants — direct damage, and the indirect costs that ripple outward from a disrupted network.
