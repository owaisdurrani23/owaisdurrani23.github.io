PhD Candidate — IUSS Pavia

When floodwater rises, which routes still get through?

I study how pluvial flooding disrupts urban transport networks, and what that means for ambulances and fire brigades reaching the people who need them — using Milan as a live case study, and green roofs as a mitigation strategy worth testing.

Focus areas

Three conditions, one network

My work compares a city's emergency accessibility across a free-flow baseline, a flooded network with no intervention, and a network mitigated by green roof implementation.

Pluvial flood modelling

Hydrological simulation of surface-water flooding and its effect on road network capacity and travel times.

Accessibility analysis

Graph-based methods measuring how ambulance and fire brigade reach times shift under disruption, with population exposure factored in.

Green roof mitigation

Testing nature-based solutions as a lever to restore network resilience and emergency accessibility — case study: Milan.
